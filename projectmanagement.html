Project Management Reviewer and Q&A

1. Overview of Project Management

Project management involves planning, organizing, and managing resources to achieve specific goals within a timeline and budget.

2. Key Roles in Project Management

  • Project Manager: Oversees the project and manages resources.
  • Stakeholders: Individuals or groups affected by the project.
  • Team Members: Execute assigned tasks to complete the project.

3. Project Management Life Cycle Phases

  1. Initiation: Define scope, identify stakeholders, prepare project charter.
  2. Planning: Develop project plan, budget, schedule, and risk assessment.
  3. Execution: Allocate resources, manage tasks, and communicate with stakeholders.
  4. Monitoring and Controlling: Track performance and manage changes.
  5. Closing: Deliver outputs, evaluate project, and release resources.

4. Key Concepts in Project Management

  • Critical Path Method (CPM): Identifies tasks that impact timeline.
  • Agile Methodology: Focuses on iterative development and flexibility.
  • Risk Management: Identifies and mitigates potential risks.

5. True/False Questions

  • The creative brief provides detailed descriptions of project tasks. False
  • Iterative rapid application development is best suited for projects with fixed requirements. False
  • Critical Path refers to the sequence of tasks that impact the project timeline. True
  • Web development projects rarely undergo changes during development. False
  • Micromanagement improves team morale and productivity. False

6. Short Answer Questions

  1. What are the main phases of the project management life cycle?

    Answer: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Monitoring and Controlling, Closing.

  2. What is the purpose of risk management in a project?

    Answer: To identify, assess, and develop strategies to mitigate potential risks.

  3. What does the Critical Path Method (CPM) focus on?

    Answer: Identifying the sequence of tasks that must be completed on time.

  4. Why is stakeholder communication important in project management?

    Answer: It ensures alignment on goals and expectations, reducing misunderstandings.

7. Tips for Effective Project Management

Focus on understanding practical applications of concepts like CPM, Agile, and risk management. Practice identifying project scenarios and selecting the best management strategies.
